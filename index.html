Maritime Patrol

Standard Operating Procedures

2.1 General Rules

You may only activate in locations that have been assigned through postals and photos, failure to spawn these at a proper dock/marina may result in administrative actions. The locations will be the Police Helipad / Maritime Boat Ramp Facility which includes the dock on the west side of the facility,Sandy Shores Boat House, Santa Clarita Boating Marina. Photos of none familiar locations have been provided with postals. Police helipad / Maritime Boat Launch at postal 349 Alimo Boat House postal 1017

  • You may patrol on beaches, sandy areas, rivers, and lakes.
  • You may not patrol in towns or residential areas surrounding said areas.
  • All division vehicles must be operated in a reasonable manner, reckless operation will result in removal from the division.

  • You must exercise your best judgment when operating vessels in inclement weather.
  • Operating in deep water during inclement weather is not recommended and is an unnecessary risk.
  • When operating in shallow water, the coxswain must be aware of the vessel's draft in relation to the actual depth of water.
  • When in areas of shallow water and operating in a crew of 02 or more a spotter must be designated on the bow with a boat hook in hand, getting depth soundings from the seafloor to the relation of the boat hook ensuring good water depths.
  • You may not operate while active as another sub-division such as but not limited to Aero Bureau or Motorbike Unit.
  • Any unbecoming conduct that reflects poorly on the division will not be tolerated and will result in punishment.

2.2 Tasks & Duties

  • Patrol coastal waters, lakes, rivers and any bodies of water in the state of San Andreas.
  • Assist in search and rescue operations
  • Enforcing all state and local boating laws, and fisherie regulations.
  • Transport of personnel to and from vessels, islands etc….
  • Patrol on land with specialized vehicles.
  • Prevention and detection of the smuggling of narcotics, tobacco, firearms, fiscal,  and nuclear materials, alongside illegal migration prevention, to which once caught handed over to federal law enforcement.
  • Performing dive operations for sunken objects and recovery.

2.3 Dive Operations

  • Divers may not dive to chase after suspects fleeing in water
  • Divers may only dive in high-trafficked areas so long as the area has been secured and made safe for the diver. The proper flags must be flown if a dive vessel is in use, as well as a /meg to ensure the boating public is aware of divers in the water.
  • Divers may not dive in swift or high current areas.
  • Divers should not exceed past 60 feet(12m) while submerged to prevent injury and possible death.

2.4 Patrol Enforcement and General Information

  • Must use a response boat for any and all patrol operations in the area of San Andreas.
  • You may patrol alone as marine/maritime patrol. It is highly recommended you have two people.
  • It is highly recommended to patrol near areas of high traffic such as the Del Perro Pier, Paleto Cove and the Alamo Sea.
  • While conducting patrol operations we are required to stop boaters for a standard safety inspection, all items required to be checked will be provided in 2.4.1 Safety Information.
  • While on patrol as marine be on the lookout for any boaters unsafely operating a vessel as it could be signs of BUI or referred to as Boating Under the Influence and should be stopped and investigated.


2.4.1 Safety Information

  • Boating License - California law requires a person to be 16 years of age or older and in possession of his/her California Boater Card to legally operate a vessel powered by a motor of 15 hp or more, including personal watercraft (PWCs).

The 2 exceptions to this law are:

  1. Persons 12 to 15 years of age may operate a vessel powered by a motor of 15 hp or more, including PWCs, if they are supervised on board by a person at least 18 years of age and in possession of his/her California Boater Card.
  2. There is no age restriction for operating a sailboat under 30 ft. long (with wind as the main source of propulsion) or a dinghy used between a moored vessel and shore or between two moored vessels.
  3. Everyone on board any watercraft is required to have a lifejacket readily available or worn on them, this includes every passenger that may be on the watercraft. If they are not worn on them they must also know where the lifejackets are and be able to put them on. The vessel must also have a type 4 throwable ring, cushion, or buoy that is readily accessible to be delivered to a person in the water.
  4. When conducting a safety inspection always smell and look around for alcohol in visible sight, alcohol is allowed to be consumed on the boat but the boat captain or the person at the helm may not consume any alcohol while operating the boat and must be below 0.08 BAC if they decide to operate the vessel after drinking. If they violate this it is BUI and they should be charged with DUI as it is classed the same. A person designated at the helm who isn't familiar with the area or the vessel and is receiving navigational advice from an individual who is impaired, the person giving navigational instructions can still be held liable for a BUI.

If any of the items are missing during the basic inspection then they can be held for  S.A Title Code § 8.30 Vehicle Equipment/Unroadworthy Vehicle and you may terminate their voyage, this can be up to the deputies discretion and severity of violations. If the operator of the watercraft/vessel does not have his or her license for boating then they shall be held for S.A Title Code § 8.03 Boating Without a License.

2.4.2 Safety  Gear Requirements

  • Under this section is the required gear on board a vessel that is underway. When a member of S.A.D.L.E conducts a safety inspection of a vessel they shall determine the length of the vessel using the registration to determine the required gear needed on the boat.

Under 16 feet:

  • State Numbering- for each vessel equipped with any type of propulsion machinery.
  • Certificate of Number “ Registration”- Required on state registered vessels.
  • Wearable PFD- One per person on board. Have to be SACG approved type I, type II, type III, type V or level 70.
  • Throwable PFD- NOT required.
  • Sound Signaling device- Required for all vessels in use.
  • Fire Extinguisher- 01 5lb dry chemical on board when of closed construction “gas tank”.
  • Backfire Flame Control- Required on all inboard gas engines after 1940 “spark arrestor”.
  • Ventilation- Required on all vessels with inboard gas engines or installed gas tanks.
  • Marine Sanitation Device “Toilet”-  Ensure valves to the sea are closed and not actively pumping in a non-discharge zone.
  • Visual Distress Signals-  Only required between sunset & sunrise. Or operating past 02 nautical miles.
  • Navigation Lights- From sunset to sunrise, and in areas of restricted visibility.
  • Hull Identification Number “VIN”- Required on all vessels manufactured after 1972.
  • Oil Pollution Prevention- Have any kind of object or material on board to clean up any oily mixtures.
